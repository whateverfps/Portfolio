THE CHALLENGE

Bring an entire municipal inspection operation into one system.

City InspectorPro was designed around a real operational problem: inspectors and administrators often work across disconnected permit exports, complaint records, facility lists, issued documents, maps, reference codes, procedures, and reporting tools. The application reframes those materials as parts of one operating environment rather than unrelated files.

THE APPLICATION

A working full-stack architecture.

  • A Python Flask application with modular route and reporting components.
  • A persistent SQLite database for application records.
  • Active and completed permit-record datasets.
  • Complaint, issued-document, facility, and manually maintained map datasets.
  • Operational dashboards and KPI modules.
  • Municipal mapping and geocode-cache support.
  • Inspection forms, complaint workflows, dashboards, and code-reference views.
  • Embedded building, fire, and administrative SOP libraries.
  • Field guides and downloadable department checklists.
  • Local Plotly assets for interactive data visualization.

THE APPROACH

Combine software, data, and institutional knowledge.

The project did not treat the dashboard, map, inspection form, SOP library, and datasets as separate products. They were organized as connected layers of one municipal operations platform.

  • Structured existing operational exports instead of waiting for perfect source systems.
  • Created modular KPI and mapping components.
  • Integrated reference documents directly into the working application.
  • Designed around daily decisions made by inspectors and department leadership.
  • Used AI-assisted development to accelerate coding, troubleshooting, and iteration.

WHY IT MATTERS

This is more than a front-end prototype.

Unlike the static Hanover demonstration, City InspectorPro contains a server-side application, persistence layer, structured datasets, reusable modules, templates, and operational reference content. It shows the ability to move from product concept into an actual application architecture.
